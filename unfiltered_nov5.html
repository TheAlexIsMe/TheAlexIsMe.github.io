Stuck Between 'Art' and Logic: College is Testing My Limits

Dispatch Log: November 5, 2025 | Data Stream: School & Theory

I am genuinely starting to question if I made the right choice coming back to school. I feel like I'm constantly being pulled in two directions: the theoretical freedom of my Digital Media classes versus the cold, unforgiving reality of general education requirements like contemporary math.

The Weight of the Digital Media Degree

On one hand, my art/media classes are intellectually engaging. We discuss semiotics, the history of glitch art, and the philosophical implications of AI ethics. I feel like I'm learning how to truly *critique* the digital world I grew up in. The professors encourage free expression and conceptual depth.

On the other hand, the degree is a piece of paper, and that piece of paper demands I pass a college-level math course. Suddenly, all that talk about conceptual critique vanishes, replaced by the sheer, brutal logic of numbers. The two sides of my brain—the analytical and the creative—are at war.

It feels like the degree is asking me to be two different people: a free-thinking artist who breaks the mold, and a standardized test-taker who memorizes formulas.

I know I need the degree to unlock the next phase, but the cost feels higher than just tuition. It's the mental energy spent trying to force a left-brained skill into a right-brained worldview. It leaves me feeling drained and questioning the holistic value of a university education when it demands such schizophrenic performance.

I just need to survive this math class. Then maybe I can return to the art of being Alex, unfiltered.

— Alex
