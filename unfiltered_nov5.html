Stuck Between 'Art' and Logic: College is Testing My Limits

Dispatch Log: November 5, 2025 | Data Stream: School & Theory

I feel compelled to write this because I'm genuinely starting to question if I made the right choice coming back to school. It's not the workload, it’s the absolute disconnect between expectation and reality in two of my key classes.

The Photography Class: When Critique Becomes Control

I came into this course expecting to learn the fundamentals, the mechanics, and the theory that underpins great visual media. Instead, I’ve been thrown into a deep end filled with "art" that I find frankly disturbing. I understand that art is subjective, but seeing some of these projects—which honestly just look like people with too much free time doing random, unsettling things—and being told this is the syllabus, gives me an eerie, off-putting feeling.

But the real issue is the grading. A long time ago, on my first assignment, I made the mistake of admitting, "You know, I kinda just did it last minute and didn't actually try." Since then, it feels like my teacher has basically been nitpicking everything I do. This is especially true for the work I find most personal or the work where I try to convey a genuine meaning.

I submitted an assignment that I genuinely believed deserved at least an 8 or 9. The grade I got back was a 2/10. That pissed me off.

It feels like there's no liberty for free art. She says she wants freedom, but the feedback implies she expects a 10/10 execution of a very specific, invisible requirement that she never articulates. If we are meant to be learning *fundamentals*, why is the standard so unforgivingly high for personal interpretation? It feels like control, not critique.

Contemporary Mathematics: The Logic Trap

Then there's my math class. Contemporary Mathematics. You expect equations, numbers, and clear problems. Instead, I'm thrown into questions of pure logic with no mathematical safety net. I feel completely blindsided.

My test scores speak for themselves: I got a 0 on my first major test because I stupidly forgot to complete the fingerprinting requirement at CASA. Then, on the second, I barely scraped a 43. I am failing the class, and it absolutely erks my soul because I see my friends—the ones I study with—doing so well. One of them pulled a 93!

The contrast is jarring. I feel like I'm putting in effort, trying to convey genuine perspective in my photography, and trying to grasp abstract logic in my math, but the universe keeps handing me zeros and twos. Watching my friends succeed while I flounder makes me look at my whole college journey and ask the scariest question of all:

Did I make the wrong choice?
