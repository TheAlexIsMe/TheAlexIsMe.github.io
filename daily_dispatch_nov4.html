Stolen Scooters, Revoked FAFSA, and the View from The Cage

Dispatch Log: November 4, 2025 | Data Stream: Life & Culture

Yesterday felt like a perfect split screen: one side was overwhelming stress, and the other was genuinely good, distracting fun. That dichotomy is basically what college life is, I guess.

The Weight of the 'Adult' Life

The **FAFSA** issue is looming. Getting that notice that my aid might be revoked because of some missing form or deadline mix-up hit harder than any midterm. It’s a constant battle to juggle classes, work, and the bureaucracy of staying enrolled. It makes me question the value of all this effort when a single piece of paper can erase the entire foundation.

And then there's the micro-aggression of campus life: someone stole one of the scooters I rely on to get across UH's sprawling campus. It’s minor in the grand scheme, but it's just another reminder of the small hurdles that pile up until you're climbing a mountain just to get to class.

It feels like the universe is simultaneously demanding perfection in school while throwing bureaucratic and practical hurdles in the way of even getting there.

The View From 'The Cage'

But the evening made up for it. My friends and I went to The Cage—that little-known bar near campus—and just completely unplugged. No talk of projects, no talk of FAFSA, no talk of the stupid scooter. Just loud music, cheap drinks, and laughing until my face hurt. It was a genuine reset button.

That's the balance, right? Finding those small, **unfiltered moments of real life** that remind you why you're fighting all the noise. The noise is constant, but so is the friendship.

— Alex
