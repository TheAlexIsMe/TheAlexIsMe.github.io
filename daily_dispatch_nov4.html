Stolen Scooters, Revoked FAFSA, and the View from The Cage

Published: November 4, 2025

Yesterday felt like a perfect split screen: one side was overwhelming stress, and the other was genuinely good, distracting fun. That dichotomy is basically what college life is, I guess, but right now the stress side is winning.

The Financial Real-World Spin

I attended class and got some assignments turned in—I know they weren't my best work, but honestly, that's where I'm at right now. The financial situation is heavy. In the last few weeks, my scooter was stolen (had to immediately buy another one), my FAFSA was revoked, which left me with an outstanding balance, and now my meal plan is gone as well. When that ran out yesterday, I realized I have about $5 left in my debit account. It’s hard to focus on critical media theory when I'm figuring out how I'm going to eat lunch tomorrow.

It’s hard to focus on critical media theory when I'm figuring out how I'm going to eat lunch tomorrow.

That said, after a necessary dose of digital media escape (Roblox, then fixing my other computer, then some late-night Fortnite), the day shifted in a way only a good night of sports can manage.

The View from The Cage

After class, I met up with my friends, and we ended up assisting with the set-up for the UH vs. LeHigh basketball game. Being in the arena is always a good time, but sitting in **"The Cage"** when we won 75 to 57 felt great. It was pure, simple victory, completely disconnected from my mounting debts.

And yes, there was this extremely hot guy sitting right in front of us. He was exactly my type—good looking, fun energy, everything. I was mad for about five seconds when I found out he was straight and had a girlfriend, but you have to appreciate the view when you get it, right? It was a final, perfect, ridiculous punctuation mark on a long day of ups and downs. That's a memory I can hold onto when I'm worrying about outstanding balances.

— Alex
